Research interests and publications

As a physicist I worked on condensed matter physics. My most recent work focused on quantum phase transitions of certain two dimensional materials, with the aim to understand the emergence of certain symmetries. The result of that research is summarized in these articles:

  • Emergent symmetries and coexisting orders in Dirac fermion systems
    E. Torres, L. Weber, L. Janssen, M.M. Scherer
    Phys. Rev. Research (accepted for publication) (2020)

  • Fermion-induced quantum criticality with two length scales in Dirac systems
    E. Torres, L. Classen, I.F. Herbut, M.M. Scherer
    Physical Review B 97 (12), 125137 (2018)

A long standing interest of mine was also the description of topological properties of two dimensional systems. In particular, the quantum Hall effect and related systems. This led me to study field theory dualities in the context of the fractional quantum Hall effect. Our findings were published as

  • Dual QED3 at Nf=1/2 is an interacting CFT in the infrarred
    D. Roscher, E. Torres, P. Strack
    Journal of High Energy Physics (11), 17 (2016)
